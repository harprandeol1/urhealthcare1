What is Direct Primary Care?

Direct Primary Care (DPC) is a modern, patient-centered approach to healthcare that removes the middlemen—insurance companies, networks, and third-party approvals—so you can focus on your health with your doctor. Instead of paying for each visit or service, patients join through a simple monthly membership that covers unlimited access to primary and urgent care.

  • Membership model: Unlimited visits, extended appointments, same/next-day access, and direct communication with your care team.
  • No insurance hassles: No copays, deductibles, or surprise bills.
  • Comprehensive care: Preventive, chronic, urgent care, many meds, basic labs, and minor procedures included.

Why It’s Different from Traditional Healthcare

In traditional healthcare, insurance companies often dictate how doctors practice—limiting time with patients and inflating costs. Direct Primary Care flips that model: your membership is a direct relationship with your doctor. This means your provider works for you—not the insurance company.

Direct Primary Care — FAQ

Is DPC the same as health insurance?

No. DPC covers primary and urgent care, many medications, basic labs, and minor procedures. Specialty and hospital services are outside the membership. Many members pair DPC with a high-deductible insurance plan.
