What is Direct Primary Care?

Direct Primary Care (DPC) is a modern, patient-centered approach that removes insurance middlemen so you can focus on your health with your doctor. Instead of paying for each visit, you join through a simple monthly membership that covers unlimited access to primary and urgent care.

Why It’s Different

In traditional healthcare, insurance companies often dictate how doctors practice—limiting time with patients and inflating costs. Direct Primary Care flips that model: your membership is a direct relationship with your doctor.

Pair DPC with High-Deductible or Catastrophic Insurance

We help you combine your DPC membership with a high-deductible (HDHP) or catastrophic insurance plan to cover specialty, hospital, or emergency needs.

  • Lower total cost: Many members see premiums and overall healthcare costs go down by ~20–25% when pairing DPC with an HDHP.
  • Coverage for the rare stuff: About 95% of everyday care is primary care—we take care of that directly. Catastrophic/HDHP plans handle the bigger, rarer events.
  • We’ll guide you: Our team can walk you through options so your plan fits your health and budget.

FAQ

Is DPC the same as health insurance?

No. DPC covers primary & urgent care (including many meds/labs/procedures). Specialty, hospital, surgery, and ER are outside the membership and typically handled by your insurance.

Are medications included?

Yes. Our covered list is included at no extra cost for members.

Do you do telehealth and after-hours?

Yes. We offer same/next-day video visits and 7-day urgent care access. After-hours messaging helps guide next steps for urgent needs.

Can I keep my specialists?

Absolutely. We coordinate referrals and share records; you can continue seeing your existing specialists.

Is there an age limit?

Membership is available for patients 65 years and younger.

How do billing & cancellations work?

Membership is billed monthly or yearly. You can cancel anytime before your next billing cycle—no long-term contract.

Do you accept HSA/FSA?

Yes — effective January 2026 DPC will be HSA-eligible (confirm with your tax advisor/plan). FSA cards are commonly accepted for eligible services.

What if I’m traveling or move?

Telehealth can help for many needs while traveling. If you move out of area, you can cancel or ask us for a handoff to a nearby DPC clinic.

Can kids join? What ages?

Yes, ages 5+. Pediatric sick/well visits are included. Vaccinations may be billed separately if obtained externally.
