Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Our on-site CBC helps evaluate infection, anemia, and platelet problems with a small blood sample.

Core Values

  • WBC (white blood cells)
  • RBC (red blood cells)
  • HGB (g/dL)
  • HCT (%)
  • PLT (platelets)
  • MCV (fL)

Differential (when indicated)

  • Neutrophils (Neut, Neut %)
  • Lymphocytes (LYMPH, LYMPH %)
  • WBC % (automated differential)

Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)

Broad overview of organ function: electrolytes, kidney, liver, and glucose.

  • General Chemistry • Electrolyte Panel
  • Kidney Panel • Renal Function
  • Liver Panel
  • Biochemistry Panel

Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)

Essential electrolytes and kidney function. Often used for dehydration, medication monitoring, and pre-op screening.

Lipid Panel

Cholesterol screening for heart disease risk. Includes total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, and triglycerides.

Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)

Average blood sugar over ~3 months to diagnose and monitor diabetes.

Blood Glucose

Point-of-care fingerstick or venous blood draw—fasting or random as ordered.

Urinalysis (Dipstick & Microscopy as ordered)

Quick test for urinary tract infection, dehydration, diabetes screening, and kidney conditions.

  • Glucose
  • Bilirubin
  • Ketones
  • Specific Gravity
  • pH
  • Protein (PRO)
  • Nitrite
  • Leukocytes
  • Microalbumin (MALB)
  • Creatinine (urine)

Spirometry

Measures lung function (asthma, COPD) and response to treatment.

ECG / EKG

Heart rhythm and rate evaluation for chest discomfort, palpitations, or medication monitoring.

Rapid Immunoassays

On-site swab tests with quick turnaround:

  • RSV
  • Influenza (Flu A/B)
  • Strep (Group A)

hCG — Pregnancy

Urine or blood testing to confirm pregnancy and guide early prenatal care.

Lab FAQ

Do I need to fast for labs?

Fasting is usually required for a lipid panel (8–12 hours) and sometimes for glucose testing. We’ll confirm instructions when we order your labs.

How quickly will I get results?

Most rapid tests are ready during your visit. Many CBC/CMP results are same day; others return within 1–2 business days. We’ll message you through OnPatient and discuss next steps.

Can I walk in for testing?

Yes—walk-ins are welcome for urgent needs. You can also schedule online for convenience.

Are labs covered by my insurance?

Coverage varies by plan. We verify benefits at check-in and explain any copays, deductibles, or coinsurance before testing.

Emergency symptoms (severe chest pain, stroke signs, major trauma, severe breathing trouble, or uncontrolled bleeding) require 911 or the nearest ER.
